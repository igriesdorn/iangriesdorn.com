CASE STUDY

Own Up Account Page Refresh

How data-driven A/B testing and customer-centric design increased shopper conversion rates by 144%

144% Increase in Conversion Rate
12 hrs Faster Time to First Connection
2 Months Testing & Iteration Period

Company Overview

Own Up is a mortgage concierge shopping service that shops against a network of lenders on behalf of customers, ensuring they get the best rate possible. By acting as an advocate for homebuyers, Own Up simplifies the complex mortgage shopping process.

The Challenge

Customers navigating the Own Up account page were experiencing confusion about their next steps in the mortgage process. Many shoppers were uncertain whether they should contact our Home Advisors (our preferred conversion metric) or reach out to lenders directly.

Core Problem: The existing account page lacked clear guidance and personalization, resulting in customer uncertainty and suboptimal conversion rates.

This confusion was creating friction in the customer journey and preventing shoppers from getting the personalized guidance that would help them secure the best mortgage rate.

My Role

As the lead Product Manager on this initiative, I coordinated efforts across multiple teams including sales, operations, design, engineering, and product. My responsibilities included:

  • Leading user research and data analysis to identify pain points
  • Defining the product vision and strategy
  • Prioritizing features using impact vs. effort scoring
  • Coordinating A/B testing methodology and analysis
  • Facilitating cross-functional collaboration and alignment
  • Driving iterative improvements based on test results

Discovery & Research

To deeply understand the problem, I employed a multi-faceted research approach:

User Research

Conducted in-depth user interviews with customers at various stages of the mortgage shopping process to understand their needs, frustrations, and decision-making patterns.

Session Recording Analysis

Analyzed user session recordings of traffic on the existing account page to identify behavioral patterns, points of confusion, and areas where users were getting stuck.

Data Analysis

Studied quantitative data inside of Looker to identify trends in user behavior, conversion patterns, and drop-off points in the customer journey.

Initial Hypothesis: We believed that by simplifying navigation and providing clearer guidance, we could increase shopper conversion rates by 30%.

The Solution

Based on our research insights, we redesigned the account page with several key improvements:

Simplified Navigation

Streamlined the page structure to make the customer journey more intuitive and reduce cognitive load.

Contextual Side Menu

Introduced a side menu that provided additional context and guidance for various tools and features available to shoppers.

Personalized Home Advisor Matching

Implemented a personalization feature that matched each borrower with a custom-selected Home Advisor best suited to their specific needs and situation.

Feature Prioritization

Used a standard impact vs. effort scoring algorithm to determine which features were most critical for shoppers in our MVP, and which could be iterated on later. This ensured we were delivering maximum value quickly while maintaining an agile development approach.

A/B Testing Approach

Rather than a single large test, we ran multiple A/B tests over a two-month period. This iterative approach allowed us to:

  • Test individual features and their impact
  • Quickly identify what was working and double down on those features
  • Course-correct on elements that weren't performing as expected
  • Build momentum and confidence with stakeholders through incremental wins

Metrics Tracked

Beyond conversion rate, we measured interactions with various tools and modals provided to end users. This helped us understand customer intent and which features were driving the most value.

Surprising Finding: We discovered a significantly higher conversion rate among customers who clicked to see more details about their Home Advisor. This insight led us to invest more time and resources into enhancing this feature.

What Drove Success?

The exceptional results were driven by our tight, agile approach to A/B testing and iteration. By quickly identifying what was working and investing more in those areas, we were able to compound our wins and exceed expectations.

Key Learnings

Voice of the Customer

This project reinforced the critical importance of always incorporating the voice of the customer into every project I lead. Ultimately, the products I build are for their benefit, and by prioritizing their needs through research, testing, and iteration, I can consistently achieve results that match or exceed targets.

Additional insights that shaped my approach to future projects:

  • Iterative testing beats big bang launches ‚Äì Multiple smaller tests allowed us to learn faster and optimize continuously
  • Personalization drives engagement ‚Äì Customers respond positively when experiences are tailored to their specific needs
  • Small details matter ‚Äì The Home Advisor detail view seemed minor, but became a key conversion driver
  • Cross-functional collaboration is essential ‚Äì Success required alignment and expertise from sales, operations, design, engineering, and product teams
